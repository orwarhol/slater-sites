A museum with children is a race against attention, not a tour. Two focused hours beat a forced four, and the goal is to leave while everyone still likes the place.

## Pick three rooms before you arrive

Look at the map the night before and choose three rooms, not three floors. Let each child claim one. Ownership turns wandering into a mission.

## Build in one snack and one bench

Plan a deliberate stop halfway: a snack, a bench, and five minutes of nothing. The pause is what makes the second hour possible.

## End at the most spectacular thing

Save the giant skeleton, the gold room, or the planetarium for last. You want the final memory to be the loudest one, and you want to leave before the meltdown, not after it.
