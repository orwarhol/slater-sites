A regional day pass is one of the few travel deals that genuinely rewards laziness: buy once in the morning and stop overthinking fares. But the math only works if you actually move.

## Three stops is the sweet spot

One long ride out and two shorter hops back usually beats a single far-flung destination. Pick a far anchor town, then two smaller places along the return line you would never have planned a separate trip for.

## Ride against the commute

Travel out of the city late morning and back in the early evening. You will dodge the crush, get a seat by the window, and see the countryside in good light both ways.

## Keep the pass somewhere obvious

Inspectors are common on regional lines. Keep the pass in the same pocket all day so you are never digging for it, and photograph it in case it walks off.
