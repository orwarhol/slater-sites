A coast out of season is a different town than the one on the postcards. Half the shutters are down, the light is sharper, and the people you meet actually live there. That is the version worth visiting.

## Saturday: walk the whole front

Start at one end of the seawall and walk to the other before you commit to anywhere. Note which café has locals at the counter and which bakery sells out by noon. You are scouting, not deciding.

## Saturday night: eat where the boats land

The honest seafood is near the harbor, not the viewpoint. Look for a short chalkboard menu that changes with the catch.

## Sunday: one harbor, no plan

Give yourself a single, unhurried morning by the water with a coffee and no schedule. The coast is best when you stop trying to cover it.
